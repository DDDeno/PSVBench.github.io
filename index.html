Abstract

Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) excel on general video benchmarks, while existing datasets primarily target natural scenes, overlooking the unique complexity of presentation-style videos. Current domain-specific benchmarks also fall short, typically relying on low-level perceptual tasks or outcome-oriented evaluations. To address this gap, we introduce PSVBench, comprising 933 videos across six disciplines, paired with timestamped transcripts and 2,030 multi-dimensional MCQs.

Through a comprehensive evaluation of 30 diverse LMMs and systematic diagnostics on modality reliance and sampling strategies, we find models rely on textual shortcuts over genuine multimodal alignment and thinking. This poses a new challenge for future native multimodal understanding.
